An **air conditioner size calculator** converts home or room square footage into BTU or tonnage — the capacity rating on every AC unit. Whether you need an **air conditioner calculator** for a single room, an **air conditioner unit calculator** or **air conditioner unit size calculator** for central AC, an **air conditioner estimate size** before getting quotes, or help **determining air conditioner size** for a whole home, the math starts with conditioned area and climate.

## Air Conditioner Size by Square Footage

| Sq ft | Estimated BTU | Approx. tons |
|---|---|---|
| 600 | 12,000 | 1.0 |
| 900 | 18,000 | 1.5 |
| 1,200 | 24,000 | 2.0 |
| 1,500 | 30,000 | 2.5 |
| 1,800 | 36,000 | 3.0 |
| 2,000 | 40,000 | 3.3 |
| 2,500 | 50,000 | 4.2 |
| 3,000 | 60,000 | 5.0 |

These assume 8-ft ceilings, average insulation, and moderate climate. Adjust up 10–15% for hot/humid zones; down 10–15% for cool zones.

A **size air conditioning unit calculator** should apply those climate and construction adjustments automatically — not just multiply sq ft by a constant.

## Central vs Room AC Sizing

**Window/portable AC:** The **air conditioner calculator** result applies directly — buy the closest standard BTU rating (8,000, 10,000, 12,000, etc.).

**Central AC:** Use calculator output as a sanity check. Final size must come from Manual J. An **air conditioner unit size calculator** that skips load calc details will oversize systems in well-insulated homes and undersize in sun-heavy rooms.

**Mini-split:** Size per zone. A 2,000 sq ft open plan may need one large head; a 2,000 sq ft four-bedroom home needs multiple zones.

## Adjustment Factors

Multiply base BTU by these when using a manual **air conditioner estimate size**:

- **9-ft ceilings:** × 1.13
- **10-ft ceilings:** × 1.25
- **Hot climate:** × 1.15
- **Poor insulation:** × 1.15
- **Large south/west windows:** × 1.10

## Signs Your AC Is Wrong Size

| Symptom | Likely cause |
|---|---|
| Cycles on/off every 5–10 min | Oversized |
| Runs all day, never reaches setpoint | Undersized |
| Cold but humid indoors | Oversized (short cycling) |
| Ice on evaporator coil | Undersized or airflow problem |

## FAQ

### What size air conditioner do I need for 1,500 sq ft?

About 2.5 tons (30,000 BTU) in a moderate climate. Hot climates or poor insulation may need 3 tons. Cool climates with good insulation may be fine at 2 tons.

### Is bigger always better for AC?

No. Oversized AC cools quickly but shuts off before removing humidity. Correct sizing delivers comfort and lower operating cost.

### How do I use an air conditioner unit size calculator?

Enter square footage, ceiling height, climate, and insulation quality. Compare the result to Manual J before purchasing central equipment.

### What is 1 ton in BTU?

12,000 BTU per hour. A 3-ton central AC delivers 36,000 BTU/hr of cooling capacity.
