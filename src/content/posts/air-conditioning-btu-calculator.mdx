An **air conditioning btu calculator** answers one question: how many BTU of cooling capacity does this space need? The same tool goes by **hvac btu calculator**, **btu calculator for hvac**, or **heating and cooling btu calculator** when it handles both loads — but cooling BTU is what sizes your AC.

## The Core BTU Formula

```
BTU = Square Footage × 20 × Ceiling Factor × Climate Factor × Sun Factor × Insulation Factor
```

**20 BTU/sqft** is the baseline for moderate US climates with 8-ft ceilings.

## Worked Example

**Room:** 18 × 14 ft (252 sq ft), 10-ft ceilings, hot climate, sunny windows

```
BTU = 252 × 20 × 1.25 × 1.15 × 1.10 = 9,975 → buy 10,000 BTU unit
```

## FAQ

### How many BTU per square foot for AC?

About 20 BTU/sqft in moderate climates. Range is 15–25 depending on climate, insulation, and ceiling height.

### What size AC for 1,000 sq ft?

18,000–22,000 BTU (1.5–1.8 tons) in moderate conditions. Hot climates push toward 24,000 BTU.

### Is a btu calculator for hvac the same as Manual J?

No. Manual J uses detailed envelope data and local design temperatures. BTU calculators use simplified multipliers — faster, less precise.

### Can one hvac btu calculator size heating and cooling?

Some tools estimate both. Heating load depends on climate zone heavily — verify heating output separately for heat pumps and furnaces.
