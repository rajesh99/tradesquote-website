**CFM calculation formula in hvac** work starts with one relationship: CFM (cubic feet per minute) is how much air moves through a duct or space. Every **hvac cfm calculator**, **air duct cfm calculator**, and **calculate cfm for room** tool applies variations of the same physics.

## Core CFM Formulas

**From tonnage (system airflow):**
```
CFM = Tons × 400 (typical residential)
```
3-ton system → 1,200 CFM total system airflow.

**From BTU and temperature change (sensible heat):**
```
CFM = BTU/hr ÷ (1.08 × ΔT°F)
```
Example: 36,000 BTU/hr with 20°F ΔT → CFM = 36,000 ÷ (1.08 × 20) = 1,667 CFM.

**From room volume (ventilation):**
```
CFM = Room volume (cu ft) × ACH ÷ 60
```
Example: 1,200 cu ft room at 6 ACH → CFM = 1,200 × 6 ÷ 60 = 120 CFM.

## CFM per Room

To **calculate cfm for room** cooling:

1. Get room load from Manual J (BTU/hr)
2. Assume system ΔT of 18–22°F across the coil
3. CFM = Room BTU ÷ (1.08 × ΔT)

| Room load (BTU/hr) | CFM at ΔT=20°F |
|---|---|
| 4,000 | 185 |
| 6,000 | 278 |
| 8,000 | 370 |
| 10,000 | 463 |

## Duct CFM and Velocity

An **air duct cfm calculator** also checks velocity:

```
Velocity (fpm) = CFM ÷ Duct area (sq ft)
```

| Duct | Area (sq ft) | CFM at 700 fpm |
|---|---|---|
| 6 in round | 0.196 | 137 |
| 8 in round | 0.349 | 244 |
| 10 in round | 0.545 | 382 |
| 12 in round | 0.785 | 550 |

Keep branch velocity under ~900 fpm to avoid register noise.

## Measuring Actual CFM

Field methods:

- **Anemometer traverse** at supply register
- **Flow hood** over register (most accurate for single outlet)
- **Static pressure + fan chart** from equipment manufacturer
- **T × A × V** (temperature factor × area × velocity) in ducts

## FAQ

### What is the cfm calculation formula in hvac?

The most common: CFM = BTU/hr ÷ (1.08 × ΔT). For whole systems: CFM ≈ tons × 400.

### How do I calculate cfm for room?

Divide room cooling load (BTU/hr) by (1.08 × design temperature drop across the coil, typically 18–22°F).

### What is a good hvac cfm calculator?

One that accepts tonnage, BTU, or room dimensions and returns CFM with velocity check for the selected duct size.

### How much CFM per ton?

400 CFM per ton is the residential default. Range is 350–450 depending on equipment and climate (humid climates often want 350–400 for better dehumidification).
