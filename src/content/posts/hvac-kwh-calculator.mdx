An **hvac kwh calculator** estimates how much electricity your air conditioner draws — in kilowatt-hours per hour, day, or month. kWh is what your utility bills, so converting tonnage and SEER2 into kWh tells you whether a higher-efficiency unit pays for itself.

## kWh Formula for Air Conditioning

```
kWh per hour = Tons × 12,000 ÷ SEER2 ÷ 1,000
```

Or equivalently:

```
kWh per hour = BTU/hr ÷ SEER2 ÷ 1,000
```

**Example:** 3-ton system, SEER2 15:

```
3 × 12,000 ÷ 15 ÷ 1,000 = 2.4 kWh/hr at full load
```

Real-world runtime is rarely 100%. Multiply by average daily run hours (typically 6–10 in summer) for daily kWh.

## Monthly Cost Estimate

```
Monthly cost = kWh/hr × run hours/day × 30 × $/kWh
```

**Example:** 2.4 kWh/hr, 8 hours/day, $0.14/kWh:

```
2.4 × 8 × 30 × 0.14 = $80.64/month
```

## What Affects Real kWh Usage

| Factor | Effect |
|---|---|
| SEER2 rating | Higher SEER2 = fewer kWh per BTU removed |
| Thermostat setpoint | Each degree lower adds ~3–5% runtime |
| Insulation and sealing | Poor envelope = longer run times |
| Duct leaks | 15–25% capacity lost in unconditioned spaces |
| Maintenance | Dirty coils and filters raise kWh draw |

## FAQ

### How many kWh does a 3-ton AC use per day?

At SEER2 15 and 8 hours runtime: roughly 19 kWh/day (2.4 kWh/hr × 8). Actual usage varies with climate and thermostat habits.

### Is an hvac kwh calculator the same as a SEER calculator?

kWh estimates electricity consumption. SEER is the efficiency ratio (BTU removed per watt-hour). You need SEER to convert tonnage into kWh.

### How do I reduce hvac kwh usage?

Raise thermostat 2–3°F, seal ducts, upgrade to higher SEER2 at replacement, and maintain clean filters and coils.
