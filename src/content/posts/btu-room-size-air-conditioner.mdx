| Room size (sq ft) | Base BTU (moderate climate) | Hot climate BTU |
|---|---|---|
| 100 – 150 | 5,000 – 6,000 | 6,000 – 7,000 |

## BTU Calculation Formula for Air Conditioner

The full **air conditioner capacity calculation**:

**BTU = Square Footage × 20 × Ceiling Factor × Climate Factor × Sun Factor × Insulation Factor**

| Factor | Value |
|---|---|
| Ceiling 8 ft | 1.0 |
| Ceiling 9 ft | 1.13 |
| Ceiling 10 ft | 1.25 |
| Hot/humid climate | 1.15 |
| Moderate climate | 1.0 |
| Cool climate | 0.85 |
| Sunny room (large south/west windows) | 1.10 |
| Average sun | 1.0 |
| Shaded room | 0.90 |
| Good insulation | 0.90 |
| Average insulation | 1.0 |
| Poor insulation | 1.15 |

**Example:** 300 sq ft bedroom, 9-ft ceiling, moderate climate, average sun and insulation:

```
300 × 20 × 1.13 × 1.0 × 1.0 × 1.0 = 6,780 BTU → buy 8,000 BTU unit
```

## Air Conditioner Unit Size per Square Foot

**Air conditioner unit size per square foot** typically runs 15–25 BTU/sq ft depending on climate:

| Climate | BTU per sq ft |
|---|---|
| Hot/humid | 22 – 25 |
| Moderate | 18 – 22 |
| Cool | 15 – 18 |

A **btu hvac calculator** should apply these automatically. Manual multiplication works for single rooms; whole-home central AC needs Manual J.

## Central vs Room BTU Sizing

**Room AC (window, portable, single mini-split head):** The **btu room size ac** result applies directly.

**Central AC:** Sum room loads plus duct losses, infiltration, and orientation. An **air conditioner capacity calculation** for a whole home is a Manual J load calc, not a single-room formula.

## FAQ

### How many BTU for a 12×12 room?

A 144 sq ft room needs roughly 2,880 BTU base (144 × 20). With average conditions, buy a 5,000 or 6,000 BTU unit — the smallest standard size.

### What is btu size air conditioner vs tons?

BTU/hr is the raw cooling capacity. Tons = BTU ÷ 12,000. A 24,000 BTU unit is 2 tons.

### Is a btu for room size air conditioner chart accurate for central AC?

For individual rooms, yes. Central systems need Manual J because duct losses and whole-home load differ from summing room BTUs.

### What btu room size ac do I need in a hot climate?

Add 10–15% to the moderate-climate chart, or use the hot-climate column above.
