**AC tonnage per square foot** is the fastest way to ballpark central air size — but the right multiplier depends on climate, ceiling height, and insulation. One ton equals 12,000 BTU per hour of cooling.

## Sq Ft per Ton by Climate

| Climate | Sq ft per ton | Tons per sq ft (approx.) |
|---|---|---|
| Hot/humid (South, Southwest) | 450 – 550 | 0.0018 – 0.0022 |
| Moderate (Midwest, Mid-Atlantic) | 550 – 650 | 0.0015 – 0.0018 |
| Cool (North, Pacific NW) | 650 – 800 | 0.0013 – 0.0015 |

**Ton air conditioner square footage** and **ton of air conditioning per square foot** rules come from these climate bands. A **square footage calculator for air conditioner** sizing should apply climate automatically — not just multiply sq ft by a constant.

**Tonnage of ac per square foot** varies with construction quality. Well-insulated homes with low solar gain sit at the high end of sq ft per ton (fewer tons needed). Poor insulation and large west-facing windows push toward the low end.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Result |
|---|---|
| Using one tons-per-sq-ft rule nationwide | Oversized North, undersized South |
| Ignoring ceiling height | Undersized rooms with vaulted ceilings |
| Sizing from sq ft only on sun-heavy rooms | Undersized west-facing spaces |
| Rounding up "to be safe" | Short cycling, poor dehumidification |

## FAQ

### How many tons of AC per square foot do I need?

Roughly 0.0015–0.0022 tons per sq ft in moderate to hot climates. A 2,000 sq ft home in a moderate zone lands near 3–3.5 tons.

### Is ac tons for square footage accurate for whole homes?

It is a ballpark for budgeting. Whole-home central systems need Manual J — two identical sq ft homes can differ by half a ton or more.

### What is the difference between tonnage for ac unit and room tonnage?

Room tonnage sizes one space. **Tonnage for ac unit** on a central system sizes the outdoor condenser serving the entire ducted home.

### When should I use a square footage calculator for air conditioner sizing?

For window units, portable AC, and budget planning. Not for final central AC equipment selection — use Manual J instead.
