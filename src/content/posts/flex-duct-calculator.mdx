A **flex duct calculator** sizes flexible (insulated) duct the same way as rigid metal — CFM plus friction rate — but with a critical difference: flex carries less air at the same nominal diameter. A **flexible duct calculator** must derate capacity or upsize one increment. A **round duct calculator** for rigid metal does not apply directly to flex without adjustment.

## Flex vs Rigid Capacity

| Nominal size | Rigid metal CFM (~0.10 fr) | Flex duct CFM (derated) |
|---|---|---|
| 6 in | 100 | 80 |
| 8 in | 225 | 180 |
| 10 in | 450 | 360 |
| 12 in | 700 | 560 |
| 14 in | 1,100 | 880 |

Flex duct carries **15–20% less** than rigid at the same diameter due to ribbed wall friction. Poorly stretched flex (sagged, compressed, sharp bends) can lose 50%+ of rated capacity.

## Flex Duct Sizing Rules

1. **Stretch fully extended** — compressed flex reduces effective diameter
2. **Limit length** — ACCA Manual D recommends max 15 ft per flex run where possible
3. **Upsize one increment** — if rigid chart says 8 in, use 10 in flex
4. **Avoid sharp bends** — use long-sweep elbows; kinks choke airflow
5. **Support every 4 ft** — sagging reduces diameter and increases friction

## When Flex Is Appropriate

- **Last few feet to register** — connects rigid trunk to ceiling or floor boot
- **Attic drops** — where rigid routing is impractical
- **Retrofit** — adding a supply to an existing room

Avoid flex for long trunk runs, horizontal spans over 15 ft, or any run you cannot inspect and support properly.

## FAQ

### How do I use a flex duct calculator?

Size for CFM at target friction rate, then upsize one diameter from the rigid-metal chart — or derate flex capacity by 15–20%.

### What is the difference between flex duct calculator and round duct calculator?

Round duct calculator assumes smooth rigid metal. Flex duct calculator must account for higher friction from corrugated walls.

### Can flexible duct replace rigid trunk lines?

Not recommended. Flex is for short branch connections. Long flex trunks sag, restrict airflow, and fail inspection in many jurisdictions.

### What round duct size for 400 CFM in flex?

Rigid chart says 10 in for ~450 CFM. Use 12 in flex for 400 CFM to maintain acceptable velocity and friction.
