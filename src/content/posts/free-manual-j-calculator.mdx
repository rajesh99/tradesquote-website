## What Manual J Calculates

ACCA Manual J (8th edition) determines peak heating and cooling load for a specific home using:

- Local design temperatures (ASHRAE weather data)
- Wall, roof, and window U-values and areas
- Infiltration rate (ACH or blower door data)
- Internal gains (occupants, appliances)
- Duct location and insulation (attic ducts add load)
- Orientation and shading

Output: BTU/hr heating load, BTU/hr sensible and latent cooling load, and CFM requirement.

Free tools often skip shading, exact window specs, and duct loss details — acceptable for ballpark sizing, not for stamped permit packages.

## Minimum Inputs for Useful Results

Even a **hvac load calculator free** version needs:

1. Zip code or climate zone
2. Conditioned floor area
3. Ceiling height
4. Insulation level (wall and attic)
5. Window area and type (single vs double pane)
6. Duct location (conditioned vs attic)
7. Number of occupants

Garbage inputs produce garbage outputs — a 2,000 sq ft home entered as "average" without window or duct details can miss load by half a ton.

## FAQ

### Is there a free manual j calculator online?

Yes. Our Manual J calculator covers residential loads with standard envelope inputs — sufficient for sizing validation before equipment purchase.

### How accurate is manual j calculation online for free?

Simplified free tools are typically within ±15–25% of a full Wrightsoft calc. Adequate for evaluating contractor proposals; use paid software for permit submissions requiring ACCA compliance documentation.

### What is an hvac load calculator free alternative to Manual J?

BTU-per-square-foot calculators and tonnage estimators — faster but less accurate. Use for single-room units; use Manual J for central systems.

### Does ACCA offer a free manual j?

ACCA sells Manual J documentation and approves software vendors. They do not host a free public calculator — third-party tools implement the standard with varying fidelity.
