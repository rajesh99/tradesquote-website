**Commercial hvac load calculation** differs from residential Manual J in scale, occupancy diversity, and ventilation requirements. A **commercial hvac sizing calculator** or **commercial hvac calculator** must account for internal gains (people, equipment, lighting), ventilation per ASHRAE 62.1, and often multiple zones with simultaneous heating and cooling.

## Commercial vs Residential Load Calc

| Factor | Residential (Manual J) | Commercial |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | ACCA Manual J | ASHRAE Fundamentals / Manual N |
| Occupancy | Fixed family size | Variable — use peak or diversified |
| Ventilation | ASHRAE 62.2 | ASHRAE 62.1 — often much higher CFM |
| Internal gains | Low | Lighting, computers, process equipment |
| Diversity | N/A | Not all zones peak simultaneously |

## Rule-of-Thumb Tons per Sq Ft

Quick **commercial hvac sizing calculator** estimates (cooling only):

| Building type | Tons per 1,000 sq ft |
|---|---|
| Office (open plan) | 2.5 – 3.5 |
| Retail | 3.0 – 4.0 |
| Restaurant kitchen | 8.0 – 12.0 |
| Warehouse (minimal load) | 1.0 – 2.0 |
| Data center | By equipment load — not sq ft |

These are starting points only. Restaurant kitchens, medical facilities, and data centers need detailed load analysis — not rules of thumb.

For residential-scale commercial (small office, retail under 5,000 sq ft), see also **[Commercial HVAC Tons per Square Foot](/blog/commercial-hvac-tons-per-square-foot)**.

## Key Commercial Load Components

1. **Envelope load** — walls, roof, windows (same physics as Manual J, larger scale)
2. **Occupant load** — ~250–400 BTU/hr sensible + latent per person
3. **Lighting load** — 1–3 W/sq ft converted to BTU/hr
4. **Equipment plug load** — computers, kitchen equipment, machinery
5. **Ventilation load** — conditioning outdoor air per ASHRAE 62.1
6. **Infiltration** — commercial buildings vary widely in construction tightness

## When to Use a Professional

Projects over 5 tons, multi-zone VAV systems, healthcare, laboratories, and any job requiring stamped drawings need a mechanical engineer or certified commercial load calc software — not a web calculator alone.

Small commercial (tenant improvement, small retail) can use simplified tools for budgeting before engineering.

## FAQ

### What is commercial hvac load calculation?

Engineering process to determine peak heating and cooling loads for non-residential buildings — accounts for occupancy, equipment, ventilation, and envelope.

### How does a commercial hvac calculator differ from residential?

Commercial adds internal gains, higher ventilation rates, zone diversity, and often simultaneous heating/cooling requirements.

### How many tons per sq ft for an office?

Roughly 2.5–3.5 tons per 1,000 sq ft for typical office space — verify with load calc for your climate and building.

### Can I use Manual J for commercial?

Manual J is residential-only per ACCA. Small light-commercial may use it informally; code and engineering practice require ASHRAE-based methods for commercial permits.
