An **online ductulator** is a digital version of the slide-rule wheel techs have carried for decades. Whether you search for **duct u lator**, **ductulator calculator**, or **mcquay duct sizer**, you are looking for the same thing: given a CFM requirement and a friction rate, what duct diameter (round or rectangular) keeps airflow quiet and the blower within its static pressure budget.

## What a Ductulator Does

A ductulator balances two limits:

1. **Friction rate** — static pressure lost per 100 ft of duct (typically 0.08–0.10 in.wg/100 ft residential)
2. **Velocity** — air speed in fpm (keep branch runs under ~900 fpm, trunks under ~700 fpm for quiet operation)

Input CFM and friction rate → output duct size. Input duct size and CFM → output friction rate. That bidirectional lookup is what separates a **duct sizer** from a simple CFM chart.

## How to Use a Ductulator Step by Step

1. **Know your CFM** — from equipment specs or a room/airflow calc
2. **Pick friction rate** — 0.10 in.wg/100 ft is the residential default; Manual D refines this from blower capacity
3. **Look up round size** — e.g. 400 CFM → ~10 in round at 0.10
4. **Check velocity** — if above target, go one size larger
5. **Convert to rectangular** if needed — equal cross-sectional area (see **[Duct Size Chart](/blog/duct-size-chart-cfm)**)

## When You Need More Than a Ductulator

A **ductulator calculator** sizes one duct segment. Manual D sizes the whole system: trunk, branches, fittings, and total external static pressure. For whole-system design, start with **[HVAC Duct Sizing Guide](/blog/hvac-duct-sizing-guide)**.

Flex duct carries 15–20% less than rigid metal at the same nominal size — size flex one increment larger or derate CFM capacity.

## FAQ

### Is there a free online ductulator?

Yes. Our duct size calculator performs the same CFM-to-duct-size lookup as a physical ductulator at standard residential friction rates — no download required.

### What is duct u lator?

It is a common misspelling of ductulator — the circular slide-rule or digital tool used to size HVAC ductwork by airflow and friction rate.

### Mcquay duct sizer vs online tools

Mcquay (Daikin) duct sizer is the professional desktop standard. Online calculators cover the same math for single-segment sizing; Mcquay adds fitting loss libraries and full Manual D integration.

### What friction rate should I use?

0.08–0.10 in.wg per 100 ft for residential supply branches. Manual D divides available blower static across the longest run — do not assume 0.10 on every segment of a large system.
