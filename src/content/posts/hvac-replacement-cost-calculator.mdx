## Typical Replacement Cost Ranges (2025–2026)

| System | Standard efficiency | High efficiency |
|---|---|---|
| Central AC only | $4,000 – $6,500 | $6,500 – $10,000 |
| Gas furnace only | $2,500 – $4,500 | $3,500 – $6,000 |
| Full AC + furnace | $7,000 – $11,000 | $10,000 – $16,000 |
| Heat pump | $5,500 – $9,000 | $7,000 – $12,000 |

Assumes 2–3 ton system, existing usable ductwork, average US labor rates.

| System type | AC-only vs full system vs heat pump |
| Tonnage / BTU | Larger = more equipment cost |
| SEER2 / AFUE tier | High efficiency adds $1,500–$4,000 |

| Region | Northeast/West Coast +25–35% labor |
| Electrical upgrade | Heat pump may need $500–$1,500 |

## Cost Drivers Most Calculators Miss

- **Permits** — $150–$500, required in most areas
- **Line set replacement** — $200–$800 if old lines are contaminated
- **Condensate drain rework** — common on attic air handlers
- **Thermostat upgrade** — $150–$400 for smart or communicating stats

### How much does HVAC replacement cost?

$7,000–$14,000 for a typical full AC + furnace replacement on a 2,000 sq ft home with good ducts. Single-component swaps cost less.

### Is an hvac cost calculator accurate?

Calculators return ranges based on national averages. Your quote varies with local labor, equipment brand, and site conditions — use output to spot outliers, not as a contract price.

### Does hvac system replacement cost calculator include ductwork?

Usually as an optional input. Default assumes reusable ducts. Add $2,000–$6,000 if ducts need replacement.

### Can I use a new hvac system cost calculator for heat pumps?

Yes — select heat pump as system type. Cold-climate rated units cost more but may qualify for higher rebates.
