**Air conditioning ton to btu** conversion is the most common unit swap in HVAC: **1 ton = 12,000 BTU per hour**. Tons appear on central AC nameplates; BTU appears on window units and mini-splits. Knowing both lets you compare equipment across categories.

## Conversion Formulas

**Tons to BTU:**
```
BTU/hr = Tons × 12,000
```

**BTU to tons:**
```
Tons = BTU/hr ÷ 12,000
```

**Example:** Convert 42,000 BTU to tons:
```
42,000 ÷ 12,000 = 3.5 tons
```

## Why "Ton" for Air Conditioning?

The term comes from melting ice: one ton of cooling historically meant the heat removal rate needed to melt one ton (2,000 lbs) of ice in 24 hours — which equals 12,000 BTU/hr. Modern **air conditioning ton to btu** ratings use the same BTU/hr equivalent.

## When to Use Tons vs BTU

| Use tons when… | Use BTU when… |
|---|---|
| Sizing central AC condensers | Sizing window units and portable AC |
| Reading residential equipment labels | Sizing mini-split heads |
| Talking with HVAC contractors | Comparing room AC products |
| Manual J output (often reported both ways) | Load calc room-by-room detail |

## FAQ

### How many BTU is 2.5 tons of air conditioning?

2.5 × 12,000 = 30,000 BTU/hr.

### Is air conditioning ton to btu the same as refrigeration tons?

Same unit definition (12,000 BTU/hr), but comfort cooling and industrial refrigeration apply it differently. Residential AC uses standard ton ratings: 1.5, 2, 2.5, 3, 3.5, 4, 5.

### How do I convert BTU to tons for my existing unit?

Divide the BTU/hr rating on the nameplate by 12,000. A 36,000 BTU unit is 3 tons.
